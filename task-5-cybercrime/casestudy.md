Case Study: Cyberbullying 
-	What is cyber bullying?
     - Cyberbullying refers to the use of digital platforms such as social media, messaging apps, or online forums to harass, threaten, or humiliate someone. Unlike traditional bullying, it can happen anytime and reach a wide audience instantly.
- How it typically happens?
   - A typical case can begin with a simple disagreement or personal conflict. For example, a college student posts an opinion on social media. A group of classmates disagrees and starts leaving negative comments. Gradually, the situation escalates: they begin sharing edited images, posting insulting memes, and sending abusive messages privately. Fake accounts may also be created to target the victim anonymously. The content spreads quickly as others join in, often without understanding the harm caused.
-	Who is usually targeted?
    - Cyberbullying usually targets teenagers and young adults, especially those who are active online or perceived as different, outspoken, or vulnerable. Victims may feel isolated because the harassment continues beyond physical spaces like classrooms.
-	Consequences
    - The consequences can be serious. Victims often experience stress, anxiety, loss of confidence, and difficulty focusing on studies. In severe cases, it may lead to depression or withdrawal from social life. Academically, performance may decline due to emotional distress. For perpetrators, there can be disciplinary action from the college and even legal consequences under cyber laws.
- This case highlights the importance of responsible online behaviour, empathy, and reporting such incidents to authorities or platform moderators before they escalate.
